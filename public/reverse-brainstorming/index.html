Reverse Brainstorming

Instead of going for good ideas and putting the pressure on, call for the worst possible ideas your team can come up with. Doing this relieves any anxiety and self-confidence issues and allows people to be more playful and adventurous, as they know their ideas are most certainly not going to be scrutinised for missing the mark.

How to Use the Tool

  1. Clearly identify the challenge and write it down.
  2. Reverse the challenge.
  3. Brainstorm the reversed challenge to generate ideas. Allow the brainstorm ideas to flow freely. Do not reject anything at this stage.
  4. Now reverse the ideas into solution ideas for the original challenge.
  5. Evaluate the solution ideas.

An example of reverse brainstorming

  1. Identified challenge: "How do we provide the best possible experience when visiting our new site?"
  2. Reversed challenge: "How do we provide the worst possible experience when visiting our new site?"
  3. Possible ideas for the reversed challenge:
    • Make it hard to log in to the site.
    • Use a hideous color scheme.
    • Only make the site suitable for one type of screen.
    • Have a lot of unnecessary text.
    • etc.
  4. Reversed ideas, i.e. solution ideas for the original challenge:
    • Eliminate log-ins.
    • Use a nice and suitable color scheme.
    • Adapt the site for different types of screens.
    • Only share essential information and be concise.
    • etc.
  5. Evaluate the solution ideas.
